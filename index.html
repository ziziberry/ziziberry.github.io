A History Of Social Sanctions at Harvard

Overview SS/What is USGSO?

Dean Khurana's Social Sanctions Definition of USGSOs
  • The Dean of Harvard College's recommendations in May 2016 for addressing discriminatory membership policies of undergraduate unrecognized single-gender social organizations (USGSOs)
  • Affects students matriculating in the Fall of 2017, i.e. the Class of 2021
  • Students who become members of an USGSO will not be eligible to hold leadership in recognized student organizations or athletic teams
  • Will not be eligible to receive the Dean's endorsement letters for fellowships requiring them
  • According to the Final Report of the Implementation Committee for the Policy on Membership in Single Gender Social Organizations in February 2017, "an Unrecognized Single Gender Social Organization is any selective-membership, singlegender organization, whose primary purpose is social, including but not limited to final clubs, fraternities and sororities, that has a membership that is comprised entirely of Harvard students and/or Harvard alumni. "
  • Separate from Sponsored Student Organizations and Independent Student Organizations
  • Members would be ineligible to hold leadership positions in Independent Student Organizations
